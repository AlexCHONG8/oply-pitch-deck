## Executive Summary

Oply is a sustainable, high-performance strand board manufactured from Empty Fruit Bunch (EFB) — oil palm waste material. Oply serves as a direct replacement for traditional plywood and MDF in furniture, construction, and packaging applications. The product is validated by FRIM and MTIB certified test results, demonstrating performance parity with conventional wood-based boards while offering superior sustainability credentials.

**Target Investment:** $12-18M USD (midpoint: $15M USD)
**Planned Capacity:** 60,000 m³/year
**Recommended MVP Location:** Sarawak, Malaysia

**Currency Convention:** All financial figures in USD unless noted. Exchange rate assumption: RM 4.40 = $1.00 USD (May 2026).

## 1. Product Overview

### 1.1 Product Definition
Oply is an EFB (Empty Fruit Bunch) strand board that converts agricultural waste into engineered wood products. Using a proprietary process optimized with PMDI resin (11% by weight), Oply transforms oil palm biomass — a waste stream from Malaysia's massive palm oil industry — into durable, versatile construction boards.

### 1.2 Key Product Attributes
- **Raw Material:** Empty Fruit Bunch (EFB) from oil palm processing
- **Density Range:** 600-725 kg/m³ (optimized at 650 kg/m³)
- **Resin Content:** 11% PMDI (optimized from samples 70 & 71)
- **Board Dimensions:** Standard 4' × 8' × 12mm sheets
- **Testing Standards:** BS EN 310, 317, 319, 311, 312 compliant

### 1.3 Product Variants
**Initial Launch:**
- Standard EFB Board (12mm thickness) — furniture & general applications
- Moisture-Resistant Variant — bathroom/kitchen applications
- Fire-Retardant Variant — construction & building code compliance

**Future Variants (Year 2-3):**
- Thicker gauge boards (18mm, 25mm) — structural applications
- HDF (High-Density Fiberboard) — premium furniture
- Laminated finishes — pre-finished market segments

## 3. Solution

### 3.1 Product Solution

Oply converts EFB waste — a free/negative-cost raw material — into certified-grade strand boards that meet or exceed the performance of traditional plywood/MDF. The product offers:

**Performance Parity:**
- Mechanical strength comparable to plywood (FRIM/MTIB validated)
- Screw withdrawal resistance verified
- Moisture resistance suitable for indoor applications
- Machinability compatible with existing wood-processing equipment

**Sustainability Superiority:**
- Waste-to-value conversion (EFB diversion from burning/landfilling)
- Carbon-negative potential (carbon sequestration in EFB)
- Local production (reduces import emissions)
- Certified through Verra VMR0009 (carbon credits)

**Cost Advantage:**
- Negative raw material cost (waste product)
- Hydropower energy advantage (Sarawak: $0.064/kWh vs. Peninsular: $0.095/kWh)
- Government incentives (100% Pioneer Status tax exemption)

### 3.2 Technology Approach

**Manufacturing Process:**
1. **EFB Collection:** 0-50km radius from plant (logistics optimization)
2. **Drying & Shredding:** Moisture reduction to <10%, fiber separation
3. **Resin Application:** PMDI blending at 11% ratio
4. **Hot Pressing:** 180-200°C under pressure (thickness forming)
5. **Cooling & Cutting:** Standard sheet sizing (4' × 8')
6. **Quality Control:** Density testing, strength verification

**Key Process Innovations:**
- Optimized PMDI resin percentage (samples 70 & 71 data)
- Energy-efficient drying using hydropower
- Minimal water consumption (dry process)
- No formaldehyde emissions (PMDI resin vs. urea-formaldehyde)

### 6.2 Quality Specifications

**Physical Properties:**
- **Density Tolerance:** ±5% (620-680 kg/m³)

- **Moisture Content:** 6-8% (final product)

- **MOR (Modulus of Rupture):** ≥25 N/mm² (BS EN 310)

- **Formaldehyde Emissions:** Zero (PMDI resin)

- **Hot Press:** 8-opening, 4' × 8' platen size (curing)
- **Cooling Conveyor:** 20 m length (temperature reduction)
- **Saw:** CNC cutting, 4' × 8' sheets (sizing)
- **Dust Collection:** 5000 m³/hour (air filtration)

## 11. Risk Analysis

### 11.1 Technical Risks

**EFB Supply Variability:**
- **Risk:** Seasonal fluctuations, palm oil mill closures
- **Mitigation:** 12-month supply agreements, buffer inventory (1-month stock), geographic diversification (50km radius)

**Process Yield Variance:**
- **Risk:** Moisture content variability, resin mixing consistency
- **Mitigation:** Inline moisture sensors, automated batching, QA sampling

**Equipment Downtime:**
- **Risk:** Supply chain delays (spare parts), maintenance backlogs
- **Mitigation:** Critical spare inventory, local fabrication partnerships, preventive maintenance schedule

### 11.2 Market Risks

**Customer Adoption:**
- **Risk:** Resistance to new material, specification inertia
- **Mitigation:** Pilot trials, performance guarantees, co-development with anchor customers

**Price Competition:**
- **Risk:** Plywood/MDF price wars, import duty changes
- **Mitigation:** Cost structure advantage, differentiation (sustainability), long-term contracts

**Regulatory Changes:**
- **Risk:** Carbon policy reversal, EUDR implementation delays
- **Mitigation:** Multi-market strategy (domestic focus), certification diversification

### 11.3 Financial Risks

**Capital Overrun:**
- **Risk:** Construction cost escalation, equipment price increases
- **Mitigation:** Fixed-price contracts, contingency budget (15%), phased investment

**Revenue Ramp Delay:**
- **Risk:** Customer acquisition slower than projected
- **Mitigation:** Pre-sales contracts, distributor incentives, government procurement targets

**Currency Volatility:**
- **Risk:** USD/MYR fluctuation (import equipment, export sales)
- **Mitigation:** Natural hedge (USD equipment, USD export revenue), hedging instruments
