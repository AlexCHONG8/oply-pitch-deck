## Executive Summary

### The Problem

The global wood-based panel market exceeds $217 billion (2024), projected to reach $349 billion by 2034. MDF and plywood alone represent a $130-190 billion combined market. Yet this industry faces structural headwinds: rising timber costs ($80-150/tonne for wood fiber), deforestation regulations (EU Deforestation Regulation effective December 2025), and supply chain fragility. Wood fiber constitutes 50-60% of MDF production cost, and this input has no sustainable downward trajectory.

### The Solution

Oply is an EFB (Empty Fruit Bunch) strand board manufactured from oil palm waste — a material that costs $0.50-3/tonne at the mill gate, compared to $80-150/tonne for wood fiber. This is a **30-300x raw material cost advantage**. The product has been tested by FRIM (Forest Research Institute Malaysia) and MTIB (Malaysian Timber Industry Board), demonstrating performance that meets or exceeds plywood:

- **MOR:** 32.2 N/mm² (within plywood range of 30-60)
- **Internal Bond:** 1.16 N/mm² (exceeds typical MDF/particle board at 0.8)
- **Face Screw Withdrawal:** 1,900-2,200 N (vs. plywood 1,100-1,400 N — **72% stronger**)
- **Edge Screw Withdrawal:** 1,400-1,900 N (vs. plywood 1,050-1,300 N — **46% stronger**)

### The Opportunity

Oply's manufacturing process is feedstock-agnostic. The same core technology can process rice husks, wheat straw, bagasse, coconut coir, and corn stover — enabling regional deployment wherever the cheapest agricultural waste is abundant. This creates a platform business model, not a single-product play.

### The Ask

Investment of **$12-18 million** for MVP plant (60,000 m³/year capacity) in **Sarawak, Malaysia** — leveraging Sarawak's cheapest hydropower (28 sen/kWh), 100% Pioneer Status tax holiday, Samalaju Free Zone port access (5-14 days to Shanghai), and 7-8 million tonnes/year EFB supply. ## 1. Market Context and Problem Definition

### 1.1 Global Panel Market Size

| Segment | Market Size (2025) | Projected Size | CAGR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Global plywood | $80.6 billion | $113.7 billion (2033) | 4.4% |
| Global MDF | $73.9 billion | $79.3 billion (2026) | 5.3% |
| Wood-based panels (total) | $217.9 billion (2024) | $349 billion (2034) | 4.8% |

The global plywood market consumed 165 million cubic meters in 2023. Demand is driven by construction, furniture manufacturing, and packaging — all sectors with structural growth tailwinds from urbanization in emerging markets.

### 1.2 Structural Pressures on Wood-Based Panels

**Deforestation Regulation:** The EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR), effective December 2025 for large operators and June 2026 for SMEs, requires due diligence to ensure products are deforestation-free. This constrains tropical plywood supply into the EU — the world's second-largest panel market.

**Timber Cost Inflation:** Wood fiber costs have risen steadily, driven by logging restrictions, transport costs, and competing demand from biomass energy. The MDF industry reported a 5% price increase in September 2025 alone, attributed to rising raw material costs.

**Supply Chain Fragility:** Concentration of timber supply in a few regions (Russia, Canada, Southeast Asia) creates geopolitical risk. Plywood prices rose 10-15% in four weeks in early 2026, according to major US producers.

### 1.3 The Structural Gap

These pressures create a widening gap between demand for panel products and the cost/availability of wood-based supply. Agricultural waste board technology fills this gap with a product that is:

- Structurally equivalent (tested and certified)
- Materially cheaper (fraction of wood fiber cost)
- Regulatory-compliant (non-timber, no deforestation linkage)
- Environmentally superior (diverts waste from burning/decomposition)

### 3.3 Optimization Path Identified

Testing revealed a clear path to improved performance:

**Best-performing parameters (Samples 68, 69, 70, 71):**
- MOR range: **35-40+ N/mm²**
- Fiber preparation: HK hammermill 1×, screen size 12mm, sieved to remove dust/flour
- Fiber length: 2.82 cm average
- Resin: PMDI, 11% (samples 70 & 71); 10% (sample 56)
- Resin temperature: No heating, 45°C
- Resin supplier: PMDI SG Buloh/SC 95%
- Thickness swelling at optimized parameters: 9-12%

**Key finding:** MOR is proportional to density. At higher density (650+ kg/m³), face screw withdrawal reaches **2,000-3,000 N** (vs. plywood 1,000-1,500 N). There is potential to optimize at lower density while maintaining performance.

### 7.2 Oply's Competitive Advantages

**1. Lowest feedstock cost.** EFB at $0.50-3/tonne is the cheapest agricultural waste available in industrial quantities. No competitor has access to a cheaper feedstock.

**2. Proven performance.** Face screw withdrawal of 1,900-2,200N is 72% stronger than plywood. Internal bond of 1.16 N/mm² exceeds MDF/particleboard (0.8). FRIM and MTIB certified.

**3. Formaldehyde-free.** Uses PMDI resin, producing a formaldehyde-free board. This is increasingly required by regulations (CARB Phase II, EPA TSCA Title VI, EU standards) and preferred by furniture manufacturers.

**4. Year-round supply security.** Palm oil is a perennial crop harvested continuously. No seasonal storage risk, no crop failure risk, no price volatility from harvest cycles.

**5. Multi-feedstock platform.** The same manufacturing process works with rice husks, wheat straw, bagasse, corn stover. This enables regional deployment without technology changes.

**6. EUDR-compliant by design.** Non-timber product with no deforestation linkage. Free to export to EU markets without EUDR due diligence burden.

## 9. Investment Thesis

### The structural cost advantage is durable and widening.

Wood fiber costs are driven by logging, transport, and increasingly regulation (EUDR, deforestation-free sourcing). These costs trend upward. Agricultural waste costs are driven by collection and logistics of a byproduct that mills currently pay to dispose of. These costs trend flat or downward with scale. This gap cannot be closed by conventional producers — it is a structural advantage of the feedstock choice.

### The addressable market is large with regulatory tailwinds.

A $130-190 billion combined MDF/plywood market is growing at 4-5% CAGR. EUDR (December 2025) will constrain tropical plywood supply into the EU. Green building mandates and ESG requirements are accelerating demand for sustainable alternatives. Consumers and manufacturers are willing to pay a "green premium" — but Oply does not need one. It is structurally cheaper.

### The technology is proven, not speculative.

FRIM-tested. MTIB-certified. MOR 32.2 N/mm² within plywood range. IB 1.16 N/mm² exceeds MDF. Screw withdrawal 72% stronger than plywood. This is not a concept — it is a validated product ready for commercial production.

### The multi-feedstock platform enables global scalability.

Same core process works with EFB, rice husks, wheat straw, bagasse, corn stover. Regional deployment model: build where the cheapest waste is. No single-feedstock dependency risk. LIGNO50N validates that rice/straw/wheat feedstocks produce viable boards — confirming the platform thesis.
