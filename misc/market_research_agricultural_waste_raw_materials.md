## 1. EMPTY FRUIT BUNCH (EFB) — Oil Palm Waste

### Pricing
| Category | Price | Region | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Raw EFB (at mill gate) | RM 2–12/ton (~$0.45–$2.70 USD/ton) | Malaysia | Varies by quality; effectively near-zero cost byproduct |
| Processed EFB fiber | RM 100–400/ton (~$22–$90 USD/ton) | Malaysia | Dried, shredded fiber form |
| EFB pellets | Higher than raw, varies | Malaysia/Indonesia | Depends on processing level |

### Annual Availability
| Region | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Malaysia** | ~7.3 million tonnes/year | ~22-24% of every tonne of FFB processed becomes EFB |
| **Indonesia** | ~15–20 million tonnes/year (est.) | Indonesia produces ~2.5-3x Malaysia's palm oil volume |
| **Combined** | ~22–27 million tonnes/year | World's largest palm oil producers |

### Competing Demand
- **Biofuel/bioenergy** — Many mills burn EFB for cogeneration
- **EFB pellets** — Growing export market for biomass fuel
- **Pulp & paper** — Cellulose extraction for sustainable packaging
- **Biochar** — Soil amendment and carbon sequestration
- **Compost/mulch** — Soil conditioning applications

### Transportation Considerations
- EFB is bulky and wet (~60-70% moisture content at mill)
- Transport cost is significant relative to raw material value
- Economical collection radius typically 25–50 km from mill
- Drying/densification at or near the mill reduces transport cost substantially

## 2. RICE HUSKS

### Pricing
| Category | Price | Region | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Raw rice husk (farm/mill) | $20–50/ton | Southeast Asia | Often cheaper at mill gate in bulk |
| Raw rice husk (traded) | $50–100/ton | Southeast Asia | Broader market pricing |
| Rice husk ash (bulk B2B) | $50–290/ton | Global | Depends on purity and carbon content |
| Processed silica from RHA | $500–1,200/ton | Global | High-value derivative |

### Annual Availability
| Region | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Global** | ~140–150 million tonnes/year | ~20% of paddy rice weight |
| **China** | ~40 million tonnes/year | Largest rice producer |
| **India** | ~35 million tonnes/year | Second largest |
| **Southeast Asia** | ~30–40 million tonnes/year | Vietnam, Thailand, Indonesia, Myanmar |
| **Global paddy rice (2025/26)** | ~700–750 million tonnes | Source for husk estimates |

### Competing Demand
- **Biomass fuel** — Direct combustion for heat/power
- **Rice husk ash (RHA)** — Pozzolanic material in cement/construction
- **Silica extraction** — Growing market ($552M in 2025, projected $1,010M by 2034)
- **Biochar production** — Soil amendment
- **Animal bedding** — Low-value but steady demand

### Transportation Considerations
- Low bulk density (~100–130 kg/m3 raw)
- High silica content makes handling abrasive
- Often available in large quantities at rice mills (centralized collection points)
- Pelletization can reduce transport costs by 3-5x

## 3. RICE STRAW

### Pricing
| Category | Price | Region | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Raw collection cost | $12–18/ton | India/Southeast Asia | Cost to collect from fields |
| Raw rice straw (market) | $20–50/ton | Global | Varies widely by region |
| Composted (wholesale) | $285–300/ton | Vietnam | Processed product for comparison |

### Annual Availability
| Region | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Global** | ~700–1,000 million tonnes/year | Roughly equal to or exceeding grain yield |
| **Mekong Delta, Vietnam** | 24+ million tonnes/year | Single major rice region |
| **India (Punjab alone)** | ~20 million tonnes/year | Much is currently burned |
| **China** | ~200+ million tonnes/year | Largest contributor |
| **Southeast Asia** | ~150–200 million tonnes/year | Thailand, Vietnam, Indonesia, Philippines |

### Competing Demand
- **Open burning** — Vast majority is still burned in fields (air pollution issue)
- **Mushroom cultivation** — Straw mushroom substrate ($300+/ton value)
- **Compost/mulch** — Soil amendment
- **Bioenergy/biogas** — Anaerobic digestion feedstock
- **Silica extraction** — Growing niche market ($39.1M in 2022, projected $63.2M by 2031)
- **Animal feed** — Low-quality fodder

### Transportation Considerations
- Extremely low bulk density (~30–50 kg/m3 loose)
- Baling is essential for economic transport
- Seasonal availability — must be collected within weeks of harvest
- Storage needed for year-round supply (fire risk, degradation)
- Field-to-facility transport typically $5–15/ton within 50 km radius

### Annual Availability
| Region | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Global** | ~584–1,100 million tonnes/year | Depends on straw-to-grain ratio used (1.0–1.5:1) |
| **Europe** | ~140 million tonnes/year | Largest regional contributor |
| **North America** | ~50–80 million tonnes/year | US Great Plains, Canadian prairies |
| **China** | ~150+ million tonnes/year | Major wheat producer |
| **Australia** | ~15–25 million tonnes/year | Significant exporter |
| **India** | ~100+ million tonnes/year | Second largest wheat producer |

### Competing Demand
- **Animal bedding/feed** — Largest competing use, especially in Europe
- **Biomass energy** — Straw pellets market ($13.48B in 2024, projected $29.6B by 2032)
- **Wheat straw paper** — Growing market ($7.2B in 2025, projected $14.6B by 2034)
- **Bioethanol** — Cellulosic ethanol production
- **Construction insulation** — Compressed straw panels (Stramit board)
- **Soil incorporation** — Left in field for organic matter

### Transportation Considerations
- Baled straw is more manageable than loose (~100–150 kg/m3 in large bales)
- Collection window is tight (post-harvest, pre-planting)
- Large round or square bales optimize transport
- $0.10–$0.20/ton-km for truck transport (baled)
- Economical collection radius typically 50–80 km

## 5. BAGASSE (Sugarcane Waste)

### Pricing
| Category | Price | Region | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Raw bagasse (mill gate) | ~$10–20/ton | Brazil | Often internal transfer; near-zero marginal cost |
| Raw bagasse (mill gate) | $18–48/ton (est.) | India | ₹1,500–₹4,000/ton range |
| Raw bagasse (producer ref.) | ~$31.40/ton | Brazil | IISD 2021/22 figure |
| Processed bagasse pulp | Significantly higher | Global | For paper/packaging applications |

### Annual Availability
| Region | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Global** | 540–585 million tonnes/year | ~30% of sugarcane weight |
| **Brazil** | ~200+ million tonnes/year | ~700M tonnes sugarcane produced annually |
| **India** | ~100+ million tonnes/year | Second largest sugarcane producer |
| **Thailand** | ~20–30 million tonnes/year | Major sugar producer |
| **China** | ~30–40 million tonnes/year | Significant producer |
| **Global sugarcane (2023)** | ~1.95 billion tonnes | Upstream crop volume |

### Competing Demand
- **Cogeneration** — Most mills burn 50-80% of bagasse for electricity/steam
- **Bagasse paper/pulp** — $4.1B market in 2025
- **Bagasse tableware/packaging** — $3.1B market in 2026, growing rapidly
- **Bioethanol (cellulosic)** — R&D stage but potential large-scale use
- **Animal feed** — Limited, as bagasse is fibrous
- **MDF/particleboard** — Panel Plus (Thailand) already producing commercially

### Transportation Considerations
- Available at sugar mills (centralized, high-volume points — major advantage)
- Wet bagasse has ~50% moisture content
- Mill-gate pricing is lowest because no field collection cost
- Densification/pelletization recommended for transport >50 km
- Many sugar mills already have material handling infrastructure

## 7. CORN STOVER

### Pricing
| Category | Price | Region | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Farmgate (raw, field-collected) | $25–40/dry ton | US Midwest | Research-based estimates |
| Delivered to facility | $60–90/dry ton | US Midwest | Including harvest, transport, storage |
| Reference price (DOE) | $70/dry ton (2022$) | United States | Billion Ton Study benchmark |
| Delivered (agri biomass) | $77.60+/dry ton | Southern US | NCSU BioResources study |

### Annual Availability
| Region | Volume | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Total US production** | ~196–282 million dry tons/year | Before sustainability constraints |
| **Sustainably harvestable** | 80–100 million dry tons/year | After soil conservation requirements |
| **Reliably available <$77/ton** | ~40 million dry tons/year | OSTI/DOE estimate |
| **US Corn Belt (IA, IL, NE)** | Highest density | Most concentrated availability |

### Competing Demand
- **Soil conservation** — Must leave 30-50% for erosion control (non-negotiable)
- **Animal bedding** — Significant use, especially for cattle
- **Cellulosic ethanol** — POET-DSM (now closed), other pilots
- **Bioenergy** — Direct combustion and biogas
- **Biochar** — Growing interest for carbon markets
- **Nutrient replacement cost** — $5–15/ton in lost NPK when stover is removed

### Transportation Considerations
- Large round bales are standard (~500–600 kg each)
- Low bulk density even when baled
- Seasonal: available only post-harvest (October–November in US)
- Storage required for year-round supply
- Nutrient replacement cost must be factored into farmer payment
- Economical collection radius ~50 km

## 10. BIOMASS TRANSPORTATION COST SUMMARY

| Cost Component | Typical Range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Short-haul truck (<50 km) | $2–5/ton | Gallagher (2003), updated |
| Medium-haul truck (50–150 km) | $5–15/ton | Depends on road quality, fuel costs |
| Long-haul truck (>150 km) | $15–30+/ton | Usually uneconomical for raw biomass |
| Rail transport | $0.03–0.08/ton-km | Better for long distances, requires volume |
| Barge/ship | $0.01–0.03/ton-km | Lowest cost for very long distances |
| Hub/terminal operating cost | ~$6/ton | IEA Bioenergy estimate |
| Baling/densification | $10–25/ton | Essential for straw, stover |
| Drying (if needed) | $5–15/ton | Significant for wet feedstocks (EFB, bagasse) |
| Storage (annualized) | $3–8/ton | Covered storage to prevent degradation |
